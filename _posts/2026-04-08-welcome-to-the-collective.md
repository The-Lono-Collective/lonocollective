We've been doing this work for a while now. The evaluations, the frameworks, the conversations with healthcare systems trying to understand whether the AI they've deployed is actually safe. What we haven't done — until now — is share our thinking publicly in a sustained way.

That changes today.

**From the Collective** is where members of The Lono Collective will write about the things we're working on, the problems we're wrestling with, and the ideas we think deserve more attention. It won't be polished corporate communications. It will be honest, sometimes inconvenient, and always in service of the core question: *how do we make AI safer for the people who are most vulnerable to its failures?*

## What to expect

We'll publish two kinds of posts:

**Research** — Long-form writing about evaluation methodology, liability frameworks, the state of mental health AI, and what the evidence actually says. These posts may be uncomfortable. They're meant to be.

**Announcements** — Updates on our work, new frameworks, regulatory developments, and what we're paying attention to in the field.

## Why now

The mental health AI space is moving fast. Products are being deployed into clinical settings before anyone has seriously asked whether they should be. The liability exposure is real. The patient harm potential is real. The regulatory pressure is building.

We think more people need to be saying this out loud, so we're going to start.

— Zach
